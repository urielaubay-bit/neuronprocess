What is an AI Agent?

An AI agent is an autonomous system that perceives inputs, reasons through a goal, selects the right tools or APIs to call, and executes actions — then evaluates the result and adjusts. Unlike a chatbot that answers questions, an agent gets things done.

Common Use Cases

Real-world examples of AI agents delivering value across industries.

Intelligent Customer Support

An agent that reads incoming tickets, queries your knowledge base and CRM, drafts responses, escalates when needed, and closes resolved cases — without a human touching routine requests.

Result: 60–70% reduction in first-response time.

Document Processing & Routing

Agents that ingest invoices, contracts, or forms from any source, extract key fields, validate data against your systems, flag exceptions, and route documents to the right people automatically.

Result: 80% less manual data entry.

Sales & Lead Intelligence

An agent that researches prospects, enriches CRM records, drafts personalized outreach, schedules follow-ups, and summarizes pipeline health for your sales team every morning.

Result: 3× more qualified leads touched per rep.

Supply Chain Monitoring

Agents that watch inventory levels, supplier feeds, and logistics APIs in real time — triggering reorders, alerting on delays, and adjusting forecasts before problems escalate.

Result: 40% fewer stockout incidents.

Financial Reconciliation

Agents that match transactions across systems, identify discrepancies, investigate root causes using audit trails, and produce reconciliation reports — daily, automatically.

Result: Month-end close 50% faster.

IT Operations & Incident Response

Agents that monitor alerts, correlate events, execute runbooks, open tickets, and notify the right team — resolving a large share of incidents before a human ever sees them.

Result: Mean time to resolve cut by 55%.
