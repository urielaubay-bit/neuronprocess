The Process Maturity Model

Six levels that describe how organizations manage and evolve their business processes — from chaos to autonomous intelligence.

1
Initial
Ad-Hoc

Processes are unstructured, undocumented, and entirely dependent on individual heroics. Outcomes are unpredictable and vary person to person.

Signs: "We do it differently every time," high staff dependency, firefighting culture.

2
Managed
Repeatable

Basic project management is in place. Teams follow similar steps and results are somewhat repeatable, but documentation is informal or incomplete.

Signs: Checklists in spreadsheets, outcomes vary by team, occasional manual controls.

3
Defined
Standardized

Processes are formally documented, approved, and trained across the organization. Consistent outcomes are expected and mostly achieved.

Signs: Process maps exist, onboarding follows procedures, first automation pilots live.

4
Quantitatively Managed
Measured

Processes are monitored with KPIs and SLAs. Data drives decisions, variation is understood statistically, and problems are detected proactively.

Signs: Dashboards for cycle time & error rates, automated alerts, SLA breach tracking.

5
Optimizing
Continuously Improving

The organization treats process improvement as a core discipline. Feedback loops are institutionalized; teams experiment, learn, and iterate constantly.

Signs: Regular kaizen cycles, RPA/BPM fully in production, process owners with autonomy to improve.

6
Agentic New
AI-Driven & Autonomous

AI agents perceive process state in real time, make decisions, execute actions, and self-optimize — while humans focus on strategy, exceptions, and oversight.

Signs: Autonomous end-to-end workflows, AI orchestrating multi-step tasks, measurable human time recaptured.

What the Assessment Covers

A structured review across five dimensions to give you a complete and actionable picture.

Visibility

Are your processes documented and visible to the right people? Can you trace what happened and when?

Standardization

Are steps defined, agreed, and followed consistently across teams, locations, and people?

Measurement

Do you track cycle time, error rates, SLAs, and cost-per-transaction? Are decisions data-driven?

Automation

What percentage of steps are automated today? Where are the manual bottlenecks and why?

Intelligence

Are AI or ML capabilities in use? Is the organization ready for Agentic AI — and what would it take to get there?
